Welcome!

This interactive tool helps you explore movie data and discover interesting patterns in revenue, ratings, and popularity across different genres.

Tip: You can always return to the visualizations by clicking the blue button in the top-left corner.

How to Use This Website

Top Navigation Bar

  • Average Gross Revenue: Shows average movie earnings by genre
  • Foreign% VS Domestic%: Compares where movies make their money
  • Over the Decades: Shows revenue changes over time
  • Rating over Revenue: Compares movie ratings with earnings

Right Side Panel

  • Click on any genre to select it (will turns blue)
  • Click Select All Button to choose all genres
  • Click Clear All Button to unselect all genres
  • Use the Year Slider to filter by time period (2000-2024)

Understanding Each Visualization

1. Average Gross Revenue (Bar Chart)

What it shows: How much money different types of movies make on average.

How to use:

  • Each bar = one movie genre
  • Taller bars = higher average revenue
  • Hover over bars for exact revenue numbers
  • Select genres to compare them

Use this to: Find which genres make the most money

2. Foreign% VS Domestic% (Dot Chart)

What it shows: Where movies earn their money, at domestically or internationally.

How to use:

  • Each dot = one genre
  • X-position= average foreign earnings percentage
  • Y-position = average domestic earnings percentage
  • Dots above guide line (dotted line) = more popular domestically
  • Dots below guide line (dotted line) = more popular internationally

Use this to: See which genres travel well to other countries

3. Over the Decades (Line Chart)

What it shows: How movie revenue changes over time for each genre.

How to use:

  • Each colored line = one genre
  • X-position = years (2000-2024)
  • Y-position = total revenue
  • Use year slider to change time range
  • Hover over lines for total revenue
  • Hover over dots for total revenue in that years

Use this to: Track which genres are growing or declining

4. Rating over Revenue (Scatter Plot)

What it shows: The relationship between movie ratings and movie total revenue.

How to use:

  • Each dot = one movie
  • X-position = movie rating (0-10)
  • Y-position = movie total revenue
  • Top-right Corner = highly rated and high revenue
  • Bottom-right Corner = highly rated but low revenue
  • Top-left Corner = low rated but high revenue
  • Bottom-left Corner = low rated and low revenue

Use this to: Find hidden gems that great movies that didn't make much money (Such as, Attack on Titan the Movie: The Last Attack)

Key Questions You Can Answer

For Example:

  1. Which genres make the most money? (Use Average Gross Revenue chart)
  2. Which genres are most popular internationally? (Use Foreign% VS Domestic% chart)
  3. Has horror movie revenue increased over time? (Use Over the Decades chart)
  4. Are highly rated movies always successful? (Use Rating over Revenue chart)

Tips

  1. Start simple: Select 2-3 genres to compare first
  2. Use the year filter: Narrow down to specific time periods
  3. Hover over everything: Get detailed information in that years
  4. Look for patterns: Notice trends across different visualizations
  5. Find surprises: Look for movies that break normal patterns (outliers)

Remember: Good ratings don't always mean big money, and big money doesn't always mean good ratings!
